The Perfect Trainer

Back to top

The Robinson R-22 in my opinion is the perfect helicopter to train for your helicopter pilot's license in. Although it seems very small, only seating two people, there is ample room to move around for an average sized person. I emphasize average because it is a small aircraft and has weight limits. For those students who find they are a bit more robust and exceed the seat weight limits, I would recommend looking at training in a Robinson R-44.

What makes the R-22 the perfect trainer you ask? Well, let me count the ways:

  1. Small, easy to fit into confined areas.
  2. Decent range, burns approximately 9 gallons of fuel an hour.
  3. Easy to handle controls.
  4. Very wide angle of view from cockpit.

Fun To Fly

Back to top

If you are reading this page then you are most likely an aviation enthusiast at least. You already long for the wind in your hair and soaring with the birds in the light blue sky. Helicopters in general will give you this joy, but the R-22 will help you get even closer.

Because they are so small they allow you to go to places larger aircraft can't. The lightweight nature of the aircraft will allow you to land with a lighter footprint. This makes the possibilities of where you can take the aircraft nearly endless, because let's face it, you're flying a helicopter! Hover in one spot, land and take off from a standstill, move backwards if you've gone too far -- whatever you want!
